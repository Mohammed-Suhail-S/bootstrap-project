Do you want to know about the most dangerous planets?



Proxima b

Proxima b looked promising when it was discovered. It orbits Proxima Centauri, a red dwarf star 4.24 light-years away from Earth. The exoplanet interested astronomers who thought that it had water and Earthlike properties.

Venus

Deadly planets do not only exist outside our solar system. We have one lurking nearby, too. Without a doubt, Venus is the deadliest planet in our solar system thanks to its sulfur dioxide - rich clouds. Those clouds prevent sunlight.

Read More

OGLE-TR-56b

Planet OGLE-TR-56b lies in the Sagittarius constellation 5,000 light-years away from Earth. It is one of the "hot jupiters" we talked about earlier. Hot Jupiters are often formed further out in their stellar systems and later move closer to their stars.

Read More

TrES-2b

TrES-2b is the darkest planet out there. It is so black that coal will appear lighter if both were ever placed side by side. TrES-2b is the size of Jupiter and located in the Draco constellation 750 light-years away from Earth. It is almost like our Sun.

Read More

OGLE-2005-BLG-390Lb

OGLE-2005-BLG-390Lb is a rocky exoplanet with a very thin atmosphere located right in the middle of our own Milky Way. All three factors made astronomers suspect that it could contain life when it was discovered.

Read More

Upsilon Andromedae b

Upsilon Andromedae b is one strange exoplanet that rotates around the star Upsilon Andromedae A, which is 44 light-years away from Earth. The exoplanet is too close to its sun, completing an orbit in just 4.6 days and one unbelievably hot.

Read More

WASP-121b

WASP-121b is one weird exoplanet. To begin, it is not spherical but looks more like a football. We mean American football and not the more popular one that Americans call soccer. But the shape is not what makes WASP-121b deadly.

Read More

KELT-9b

Exoplanet KELT-9b is the hottest planet we have ever found. The temperature of its star is around 9,700 degrees Celsius (17,500*F), which is almost two times the 5,480-degree-Celsius (9,900*F) surface temperature of our Sun.

Read More

CoRoT-7b

In February 2009, astronomers operating the Convection, Rotation and Planetary Transits (CoRoT-7b) satellite discovered a new exoplanet in the Monoceros constellation 480 light-years away from Earth. They called it CoRoT-7b.

Read More

HD 189733b

In 2005, astronomers discovered a Jupiter-sized exoplanet, HD 189733b, with a distinctive Earthlike blue tint 63 light-years away. However, unlike Earth, HD 189733b was not blue because of its seas, silicate-rich clouds and oceans.
